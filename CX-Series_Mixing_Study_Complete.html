📖 Fundamental Principle: Why Mixing Works

Core Concept: When you mix patient plasma with normal plasma, you dilute both the patient's deficiency AND any inhibitor present. The KEY is that the outcomes are different depending on which problem exists.

The Logic Behind Mixing Study

Scenario 1: Patient Has Factor Deficiency

  • What's happening: Patient's Factor VIII is 10% (normal is 80-120%). Their blood can't clot properly.
  • Add normal plasma: Normal plasma has Factor VIII at 100%
  • After 1:1 mixing: Factor VIII activity becomes ~50% (average of 10% + 100%)
  • Result: CLOTTING TIME NORMALIZES because 50% factor activity is therapeutic level
  • Interpretation: ✅ CORRECTED = Factor Deficiency

Scenario 2: Patient Has Inhibitor (e.g., Lupus Anticoagulant)

  • What's happening: Patient has antibodies attacking phospholipids → blocks coagulation cascade
  • Add normal plasma: Normal plasma has normal factors, BUT inhibitor is in patient's plasma
  • The problem: The inhibitor ALSO attacks the normal factors you just added!
  • Result: CLOTTING TIME STAYS PROLONGED because inhibitor inactivates normal factors too
  • Interpretation: ❌ NOT CORRECTED = Inhibitor Present
Key Insight: A deficiency can be "fixed" by adding normal plasma. An inhibitor CANNOT be fixed because it attacks the newly added normal factors too. This is the fundamental principle that makes mixing study diagnostic.

🔄 Complete Procedure: CX-9000 7-Step Process

Why 7 Steps? Why Not Just Mix Once?

Some inhibitors are "time-dependent" - they only develop after incubation at body temperature. A 1-step mixing study would MISS these developing inhibitors. CX-9000's 7-step process catches everything.

1

Patient Sample Loading & APPT1 Test

Action: Load patient plasma, run normal APPT test

What you get: APPT1 = Patient's baseline clotting time (usually 50-65 seconds if prolonged)

Why: Confirms APPT is abnormal and mixing study is indicated

2

Normal Pooled Plasma (NPP) Preparation

Action: Analyzer automatically prepares fresh normal plasma pool

What's in NPP: All coagulation factors at 100% activity, NO inhibitors

3

1:1 Mix Preparation - Immediate Correction Test

Action: Analyzer mixes exactly 50% patient plasma + 50% normal plasma

CX-9000 Advantage: Ensures EXACT 1:1 ratio - no human error in manual mixing

4

Immediate Mix Tests (APPT2 & APPT3)

Calculation: RI_CT = [(APPT3-APPT2)/APPT1] × 100%

Interpretation: RI_CT < 11% = CORRECTED | RI_CT > 11% = NOT CORRECTED

5

Immediate Correction Interpretation

If corrects: Might be factor deficiency (but need incubation test)

If doesn't correct: Likely inhibitor (but need incubation test)

6

Extended Incubation: Time-Temperature Dependency Check

Action: Mixed plasma incubated at 37°C for UP TO 120 MINUTES (2 hours)

CX-9000 Advantage: Competitors can only do 12-15 minutes. CX-9000 FULL 2 HOURS!

Clinical significance: Catches developing Factor VIII antibodies

7

Post-Incubation Tests (APPT6 & APPT7) + Final Diagnosis

Calculation: RI_ICT = [(APPT7-APPT6)/APPT4] × 100%

Time check: If (APPT7 - APPT6) > 3 seconds = time-dependent inhibitor

Automatic Diagnosis: Analyzer generates final diagnosis automatically

📊 Complete Interpretation Guidelines

Outcome 1: CORRECTS IMMEDIATELY & STAYS CORRECTED ✅

Results: RI_CT < 11%, RI_ICT < 11%, APPT7 - APPT6 < 3 seconds

Diagnosis: FACTOR DEFICIENCY (likely hemophilia)

Treatment: Factor replacement therapy

Outcome 2: DOES NOT CORRECT ❌

Results: RI_CT > 11%, RI_ICT > 11%, APPT7 - APPT6 ≤ 3 seconds

Diagnosis: IMMEDIATE-ACTING INHIBITOR (Lupus anticoagulant, direct thrombin inhibitor)

Action: Specialist consultation, specific inhibitor testing

Outcome 3: CORRECTS THEN DETERIORATES 🔴

Results: RI_CT < 11%, RI_ICT > 11%, APPT7 - APPT6 > 3 seconds (WORSENS!)

Diagnosis: TIME-DEPENDENT INHIBITOR (Developing Factor VIII antibody)

CRITICAL: URGENT Factor VIII inhibitor testing - this catches emerging inhibitors others miss!

Outcome 4: PARTIALLY CORRECTS ⚠️

Results: RI_CT between 0-20% (partial correction)

Diagnosis: COMBINED DEFECT (Both deficiency AND inhibitor)

Complexity: Requires full factor panel + inhibitor testing

💡 Real Clinical Example: Time-Dependent Inhibitor

Patient: Hemophilia A patient, Factor VIII replacement therapy
  • APPT1 (Patient alone): 58 seconds (PROLONGED)
  • Mix preparation: 50% patient + 50% normal plasma
  • APPT2/3 (Immediate mix): 38 seconds (IMPROVED!)
  • RI_CT: ~3% (< 11%) ✅ CORRECTS IMMEDIATELY
  • First impression: "Looks like factor deficiency"
  • BUT THEN... Incubate mixed plasma 120 minutes at 37°C...
  • APPT6/7 (After incubation): 48-50 seconds (MUCH WORSE!)
  • RI_ICT: ~25% (> 11%) ❌ DOES NOT CORRECT AFTER
  • ΔAPPT: 50 - 38 = 12 seconds (> 3 seconds) 🔴 SIGNIFICANTLY WORSE
  • FINAL DIAGNOSIS: TIME-DEPENDENT INHIBITOR (DEVELOPING FACTOR VIII ANTIBODIES)
  • CRITICAL ACTION: URGENT Factor VIII inhibitor titer - this patient needs alternative therapy (FEIBA or Novoseven) instead of Factor VIII
  • Why CX-9000 matters: Competitors with 15-min incubation would MISS this - results would look like simple factor deficiency. CX-9000's 2-hour incubation catches the emerging inhibitor before it causes serious bleeding.
