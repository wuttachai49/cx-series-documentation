🔬 Optical Detection System

Three Detection Methods Available

1. Threshold Detection

How: Detects when absorbance crosses a preset threshold | Speed: Fastest | Use: Quick screening

2. 1st Derivative (Rate Detection)

How: Measures rate of absorbance change (dA/dt) | Sensitivity: High | Use: Standard coagulation testing

3. 2nd Derivative (Acceleration Detection) ⭐ CX-9000 Only

How: Detects inflection point of fibrin polymerization (d²A/dt²) | Sensitivity: Very High | Use: APPT, mixed studies - catches subtle defects

Why These Wavelengths?

  • 405nm: Optimal fibrin absorbance peak - provides strongest optical contrast
  • 545-575nm: Reference wavelength to detect sample interference (hemolysis, lipemia, icterus)
  • 660nm: Extended measurement range for alternative assays
  • 800nm (CX-9000): Near-infrared provides superior penetration in hemolytic/icteric samples

🧪 Seven Parameters: Detection Methods & Wavelengths

1. PT (Prothrombin Time)

Clinical: Extrinsic + common pathway (Factors VII, X, V, II)

Detection Method: 1st Derivative | Normal Range: 10-13 seconds

CX-6000: 405nm + 575nm reference
CX-9000: 405nm + 545nm optimized

How It Works: Tissue Factor triggers extrinsic cascade → fibrin formation increases 405nm absorbance → 1st Derivative detects peak rate change

2. APPT (Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time)

Clinical: Intrinsic + common pathway (Factors XII, XI, IX, X, V, II)

Detection Method: 2nd Derivative (CX-9000) / 1st Derivative (CX-6000) | Normal Range: 25-35 seconds

CX-6000: 405nm + 660nm
CX-9000: 405nm + 545nm + 800nm (hemolytic samples)

How It Works: Partial thromboplastin + activator → intrinsic pathway → 2nd Derivative detects earliest fibrin formation (most sensitive method)

CX-9000 Advantage: 2nd Derivative catches mild clotting defects. 800nm handles hemolytic samples others fail on.

3. Fibrinogen

Clinical: Clotting factor I quantification (2-4 g/L normal)

Detection Method: 1st Derivative | Measurement: Kinetic rate

Both Models: 405nm primary measurement

How It Works: Direct thrombin converts fibrinogen to fibrin → 405nm absorbance rate = fibrinogen concentration

4. Thrombin Time (TT)

Clinical: Fibrinogen quality + quantity / Direct thrombin inhibitor detection

Detection Method: 1st Derivative | Normal Range: 11-16 seconds

Both Models: 405nm primary

How It Works: Thrombin directly converts fibrinogen → 405nm detects fibrin formation rate

5. D-Dimer

Clinical: Fibrin degradation marker / Thromboembolism rule-out (< 0.5 µg/mL normal)

Detection Method: Immunoturbidimetric 1st Derivative

Both Models: 405nm immunoassay measurement

How It Works: Monoclonal antibodies bind D-Dimer → particle agglutination → 405nm absorbance = D-Dimer concentration

6. FDP (Fibrin Degradation Products)

Clinical: Active fibrinolysis marker / DIC, sepsis, liver disease (< 5 µg/mL normal)

Detection Method: Immunoturbidimetric 1st Derivative

Both Models: 405nm immunoassay measurement

How It Works: FDP-specific antibodies bind fragments → particle clumping → 405nm detects agglutination

7. AT-III (Antithrombin III)

Clinical: Natural anticoagulant / Thrombosis risk assessment (80-120% normal)

Detection Method: Chromogenic substrate assay

Both Models: 405nm colorimetric measurement

How It Works: AT-III inhibits thrombin → uninhibited thrombin cleaves chromogenic substrate → yellow color at 405nm (inverse measurement)

🔄 Automated APPT Mixing Study

CX-9000: 7-Step Automated Process

Differentiates factor deficiency from inhibitors with 120-minute incubation - the only automated system with full 2-hour capability.

Workflow:
  1. Patient APPT baseline test (APPT1)
  2. Normal plasma pool preparation
  3. 1:1 mix preparation (precise automated ratio)
  4. Immediate mix testing (APPT2 & APPT3) → RI_CT calculation
  5. Immediate interpretation (< 11% = corrects)
  6. Extended 120-minute incubation at 37°C (CX-9000 exclusive feature)
  7. Post-incubation testing (APPT6 & APPT7) → automatic diagnosis

Clinical Outcomes Detected

  • ✅ Corrects & Stays Corrected: Factor deficiency (hemophilia)
  • ❌ Doesn't Correct: Immediate-acting inhibitor (lupus anticoagulant)
  • 🔴 Corrects Then Worsens: Time-dependent inhibitor (developing Factor VIII antibody) - CRITICAL DISCOVERY
CX-9000 Clinical Advantage: 120-minute incubation catches time-dependent inhibitors. Competitors with 12-15 minute incubation would MISS these developing inhibitors - a patient safety advantage.

🧬 Coagulation Cascade Pathways Measured

Extrinsic Pathway (PT - 3-4 steps)

Tissue Factor → Factor VII → Factor X → Factor V → Factor II → Fibrin (FAST - ~11 seconds)

Intrinsic Pathway (APPT - 6-7 steps)

Contact Factors → Factor XII → Factor XI → Factor IX → Factor X → Factor V → Factor II → Fibrin (LONGER - ~30 seconds)

Common Pathway (shared)

Factor X → Factor V (Prothrombin Activator) → Factor II (Prothrombin) → Thrombin → Fibrinogen → Fibrin Clot

Clinical Insight: PT tests extrinsic/common (fast), APPT tests intrinsic/common (slower, more sensitive to deficiencies), so APPT is the more sensitive screening test.
