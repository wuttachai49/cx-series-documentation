🎯 CX-9000 Exclusive: Automated APPT Mixing Study - Detailed

Why This Matters: APPT mixing study is a critical test for diagnosing complex coagulation problems. CX-9000 is the ONLY analyzer on the market with fully automated 7-step mixing study - your competitors are still doing it manually!

What Is APPT Mixing Study?

When a patient has prolonged APPT (slow blood clotting), doctors need to know: Is it a factor deficiency (treatable) or an inhibitor (requires different treatment)? The mixing study answers this question by mixing patient blood with normal blood and seeing if clotting time improves.

Step-By-Step: How CX-9000 Does It Automatically

1
Patient Sample Loading & Initial Test (APPT1)
What happens: Patient plasma is loaded into the analyzer and tested normally.
Why: Confirms APPT is prolonged and mixing study is needed.
Time: ~30 seconds
2
Normal Pooled Plasma (NPP) Preparation
What happens: Analyzer automatically prepares fresh normal plasma from quality control pool.
Why: Normal plasma contains all coagulation factors at 100% activity.
Key: This is the "reference" to see if patient factors can be corrected.
3
1:1 Mix Preparation - Immediate Test
What happens: Analyzer automatically mixes patient plasma + normal plasma in exact 1:1 ratio.
Why: When normal plasma is added, if patient has low factors, their activity should rise to ~50% (therapeutic level).
Result: If clotting time corrects, suggests factor deficiency. If doesn't correct, suggests inhibitor.
4
Immediate Mix Test (APPT2, APPT3)
What happens: Mixed sample is tested immediately (within 5 minutes of mixing).
Results: APPT2 (first test) and APPT3 (second test for reliability check).
Analyzer calculates: Correction Index (RI_CT) = [(APPT3-APPT2)/APPT1] × 100%
5
Interpretation: Immediate Correction Decision
The Magic Number: 11%
• If RI_CT < 11% → CORRECTED (factor deficiency)
• If RI_CT > 11% → NOT CORRECTED (inhibitor present)
But wait... Some inhibitors only appear after time/temperature. CX-9000 doesn't stop here!
6
Incubation: Time-Temperature Dependency Check
What happens: Mixed plasma is incubated at 37°C (body temperature) for up to 120 MINUTES (2 hours).
Why: Some inhibitors (like acquired Factor VIII antibodies) are "time-dependent" - they don't show up immediately but develop over time.
This is where CX-9000 beats competitors: Most analyzers can only incubate 12-15 minutes. CX-9000 goes full 2 hours!
7
Post-Incubation Test (APPT6, APPT7)
What happens: After 120-minute incubation, mixed plasma is tested again (APPT6 and APPT7).
Analyzer calculates: RI_ICT = [(APPT7-APPT6)/APPT4] × 100%
Also measures: Time difference between APPT7 and APPT6 (looks for >3 second difference for time-dependent inhibitors)
Final Diagnosis: Analyzer automatically determines if inhibitor is time-dependent or immediate-acting.
